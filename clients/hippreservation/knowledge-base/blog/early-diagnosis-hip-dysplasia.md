## What Is Hip Dysplasia?

Hip dysplasia occurs when the hip socket (acetabulum) does not fully cover the femoral head (ball of the thigh bone), leading to joint instability, pain, and potential mobility issues. While often present at birth, hip dysplasia can also develop as a child grows. Early diagnosis is critical to managing and treating this condition effectively, particularly in infants and young children.

Children diagnosed and treated early often grow up with normal hip function, allowing them to lead an active, pain-free life. When untreated or diagnosed late, hip dysplasia can lead to:

- Early-onset arthritis

### 4. Enhancing Quality of Life

Late-diagnosed or untreated hip dysplasia can result in chronic hip pain, reduced mobility, and difficulty with daily activities. Early detection allows for timely intervention, ensuring better hip function and an improved quality of life.
