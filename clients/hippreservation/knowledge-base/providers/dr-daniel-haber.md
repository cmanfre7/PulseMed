## Dr. Daniel Haber, MD

Dr. Daniel Haber is a Harvard-trained, board-certified orthopedic surgeon specializing in shoulder, hip, and knee injuries. He practices at Panorama Orthopedics & Spine Center, where he provides advanced care for athletes and active individuals of all ages. Dr. Haber offers both surgical and non-surgical treatments tailored to each patient's unique goals, focusing on minimally invasive and arthroscopic procedures that promote faster recovery and long-term joint health.

### Specializations

Dr. Haber's clinical expertise includes:

- Labrum and rotator cuff repairs
- Total shoulder replacement
- ACL reconstruction
- Patellofemoral stabilization
- Cartilage restoration
- Meniscal preservation
- Hip arthroscopy for labral tears and impingement

His approach combines state-of-the-art techniques with a personalized care philosophy—prioritizing conservative treatment whenever possible and employing surgical solutions only when necessary to restore optimal performance and function.

### Team Physician Roles

Dr. Haber serves as a team physician for:

- U.S. Men's and Women's Ski and Snowboard Teams
- University of Denver men's and women's soccer teams

He has also cared for athletes across professional leagues including the NFL, MLB, NHL, NBA, and MLS, and continues to advance sports medicine techniques that help patients return safely to their active lifestyles.

### Education & Training

- **Undergraduate:** Brigham Young University (summa cum laude)
- **Medical School:** Dartmouth Medical School (inducted into Mosenthal Surgical Honor Society, received the Freddy H. Fu Outstanding Medical Student in Orthopaedics Award)
- **Residency:** Harvard Combined Orthopaedic Residency Program (Massachusetts General Hospital, Brigham and Women's Hospital, Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, and Boston Children's Hospital)
- **Fellowship:** Orthopaedic Sports Medicine at the Steadman Clinic and Steadman Philippon Research Institute in Vail, Colorado

### Research & Recognition

Dr. Haber has published numerous peer-reviewed articles, authored textbook chapters, developed new surgical techniques, and presented his research at national and international orthopedic conferences. He serves on the Council of Delegates for the American Orthopaedic Society for Sports Medicine (AOSSM) and is an active member of the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons (AAOS) and the Arthroscopy Association of North America (AANA).

He has been recognized as a 5280 Magazine Top Doctor and continues to advance sports medicine through his clinical practice, research, and educational leadership.
