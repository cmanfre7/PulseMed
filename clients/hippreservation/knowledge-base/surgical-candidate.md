## Find Out if You're a Candidate for Hip Preservation Surgery

This quiz helps determine if you may be a candidate for hip preservation surgery. Our team uses your responses to provide personalized guidance about your next steps.

### Assessment Questions

**Sports and Activity:**
- What sport(s) do you participate in? (Soccer, Tennis, Football, Basketball, Baseball, Gymnastics, Track & Field, Dance, Hockey, Figure Skating, Martial Arts, Other)
- Has your hip issue affected your ability to perform or compete?
  - No, I can still perform at my usual level
  - Yes, slightly
  - Yes, it limits my performance
  - Yes, I've had to stop participating in my sport

**Diagnosis:**
- Have you been diagnosed with a specific hip condition?
  - Yes, by a doctor
  - No, but I suspect something like dysplasia or FAI
  - No, I haven't seen a doctor yet

**Pain and Symptoms:**
- Have you experienced hip pain or injury related to sports or activity?
  - Yes, recently
  - Yes, in the past
  - No pain, but I've been told something is wrong
  - No, but I'm concerned about my hip alignment or performance

- How long have you had hip pain, stiffness, or instability?
  - Less than 1 month
  - 1-3 months
  - 3-6 months
  - More than 6 months

**Previous Treatments:**
- What treatments have you already tried? (Select all that apply)
  - Rest or modified activity
  - Physical therapy
  - Anti-inflammatory medication
  - Injections (steroid or other)
  - Alternative therapies (chiro, acupuncture, etc.)
  - Nothing yet
  - Other

**Age:**
- How old are you? (Since age plays a major role in PAO and hip preservation planning)
  - Under 15
  - 15–18
  - 19–25
  - 26–35
  - Over 35

**Surgical History:**
- Have you had any previous surgeries or procedures on your hip?
  - Yes, major surgery (e.g., PAO, arthroscopy)
  - Yes, minor procedure (e.g., injection, scope)
  - No, never

**Timeline:**
- How soon are you hoping to get evaluated or treated?
  - As soon as possible
  - Within the next 1–3 months
  - I'm exploring options right now
  - Not ready yet, just gathering information

**Goals:**
- What's your main goal?
  - Return to my sport
  - Prevent future arthritis or hip replacement
  - Get a diagnosis
  - Avoid surgery if possible
  - I'm not sure yet

### What Happens Next

After submitting the quiz, someone from our Hip Preservation Team will contact you to discuss your results and potential next steps. This may include:

- Scheduling a consultation
- Reviewing your imaging
- Discussing treatment options
- Answering any questions you have about hip preservation surgery

### Who Is Typically a Good Candidate?

Good candidates for hip preservation surgery often:
- Are under 40 years old (though exceptions exist)
- Have a diagnosis of hip dysplasia, FAI, or labral tears
- Have minimal to no arthritis on imaging
- Have tried conservative treatments without lasting relief
- Are motivated to follow a structured rehabilitation program
- Want to preserve their natural hip joint
